Your productivity stack isn't helping you. It's a tax on your team's attention.

## The Promise of a New Tool, The Pain of a New Silo

Every product manager knows the feeling. A new problem emerges—better whiteboarding, cleaner roadmaps, more dynamic documentation—and a new SaaS tool promises a solution. It’s easy to sign up for a free trial. It’s easy to get a few team members excited. Soon, you’re paying $20 a month per seat for another specialized app.

Repeat this a dozen times and you haven’t built a productivity engine. You’ve built a collection of disconnected information silos.

The spec in Notion falls out of sync with the tasks in Asana. The decisions made in a Slack huddle never make it back to the Jira epic. The real cost isn’t the line item on the finance report. It’s the cognitive overhead. Every time an engineer has to ask, “Is this Figma comment or the Jira description the final word?” you burn a little bit of focus. That friction, compounded across a whole team and a whole quarter, is devastating.

## How to Spot the Sickness

Is your tool stack bloated? Ask yourself and your team these questions.

*   **When someone asks for the "latest version," what's the answer?** If it involves pointing to three different URLs and a Slack search query, you have a problem. There should be one, and only one, answer.
*   **Are you a human API?** Do you spend your time copying and pasting screenshots from one tool into a presentation in another? Are you manually re-typing action items from meeting notes into a project management app? Your job is strategy, not data entry.
