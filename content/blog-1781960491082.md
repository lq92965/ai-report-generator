I spent a Tuesday afternoon staring at our main competitor’s marketing site. It was a sea of optimistic jargon: "seamless integration," "next-generation platform," "empowering your enterprise." I learned nothing. Their press releases were just as useless, touting a new funding round and quoting a happy customer.

This is the standard competitive analysis playbook. We look at the shiny exterior—the marketing copy, the G2 reviews, the conference talks. But we’re looking in the wrong place.

A company’s true product strategy isn’t in what they promise. It’s in what their product actually *does*. And the most honest, detailed, and up-to-date description of what a product does is buried in the one place nobody ever looks: the help documentation.

Your competitor's user guides, API docs, and troubleshooting articles are a goldmine. They are the ground truth. And with a modern AI, you can turn that mountain of boring text into a brutally honest competitive strategy document in about 30 minutes.

### Step 1: Stop Guessing, Start Gathering

First, you need the raw material. Forget the homepage. You're looking for the "Support," "Help Center," or "Developers" links, usually tucked away in the footer.

Your goal is to collect the text from their core documentation. This includes:
*   **Getting Started Guides:** How do they onboard a new user? This reveals their target user's initial pain point.
*   **Feature Explanations:** How do they describe the step-by-step process for using their key features? This shows you the intended workflows.
*   **API Documentation:** This is the blueprint. It shows you exactly what data they work with and what integrations are possible.
*   **Troubleshooting & FAQs:** This is where the product's sharp edges are. What are users constantly getting stuck on? This is a list of their product's weaknesses and your opportunities.

You don’t need a fancy web scraper. For most help centers, you can simply copy and paste the text from a dozen or so key articles into a single document. If it’s a massive site, a browser extension like Simple Scraper can do the job. Just get the text.

### Step 2: Find an AI With a Big Brain

You need an AI that can handle a massive amount of text. Pasting 20,000 words into a standard chatbot will fail. You need a model with a large "context window." This means it can read and analyze the equivalent of a short novel all at once.

As of today, the best tools for this are:
*   **Claude 3 (Sonnet or Opus):** Excellent at handling long documents and pulling out nuanced insights.
*   **Gemini 1.5 Pro:** Has an enormous context window and is great for finding specific details within a wall of text.

The specific tool matters less than the capability. Find one that can take your entire document of scraped text in a single prompt.
