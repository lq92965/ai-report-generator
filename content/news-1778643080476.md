On the same day SpaceX showed off new renders of Starship V3, a machine designed to carry humanity to Mars, the U.S. government quietly created a new billing mechanism for Medicare. One is a story of fire, steel, and interplanetary ambition. The other is a story of bureaucratic paperwork. One of them might actually change more lives in the next five years.

This is the strange duality of technology right now. The headlines are dominated by spectacle—gleaming rockets and multi-billion dollar funding rounds like the $450 million just raised by Kevin Hartz’s A* fund. The money flows toward AI applications and bold promises. But the real, grinding work of progress happens elsewhere, in places we rarely look. It happens in the obscure corners of the Linux kernel, where a seemingly clever optimization to save power when a system is "idle" can create a death spiral for the internet protocols that run our modern world, as engineers at Cloudflare just discovered.
